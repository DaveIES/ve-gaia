Renewable Energy Use

The LZCT review provides an initial indication of the potential for carbon reduction using low or zero carbon technologies.

Use the energy hierarchy

  • Be Lean (reduce)
  • Be Green (renewables)
  • Be Clean (efficient supplies)

Low carbon v renewables

Low carbon technologies e.g. CHP could deliver a better overall result than renewables in many real situations. The review highlights the carbon and energy headlines in the model data because they tell different sides of the story

Typically heat demand is easier to tackle than electrical demand, but always address efficiency and optimisation before considering LZCT, which should be considered in the context of the following preference hierarchy:

  1. Passive design
  2. Solar DHW
  3. Renewable Tri/co generation
  4. Tri/co generation
  5. Community heat cool networks
  6. Ground heat pumps
  7. Heat pumps
  8. Gas condensing heat
  9. Traditional heat

Remember that solutions that address small carbon problems yield small reductions

Indicative potential

The review indicates the potential of possible LZCT; reasonable assumptions have been made but you should not consider this analysis a design. Be aware that there are practical and economic limits on the size of most LZCT.

Factors that affect such limits are:

  • Building type
  • Load profile
  • Load balance (heat/power)
  • Technology characteristics
  • Network export costs
